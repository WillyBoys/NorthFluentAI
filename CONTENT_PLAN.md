# CONTENT_PLAN.md

## Purpose

This document defines the initial content strategy for NorthFluent AI so that content creation stays aligned with the mission and MVP scope.

## Content Goals

Every page should help beginners do at least one of the following:
- Understand AI more clearly
- Use AI more effectively
- Recognize where AI needs human oversight
- Build confidence without building blind trust

## Core Content Pillars

All content should fit into one or more of these pillars.

### 1. AI Basics
Explain the core ideas of AI in plain language.

Examples:
- What AI is
- What machine learning means
- What generative AI is
- How AI produces outputs

### 2. Practical Use Cases
Show how beginners can use AI in everyday life.

Examples:
- Studying
- Writing help
- Brainstorming
- Summarizing
- Job prep
- Productivity tasks

### 3. Prompting Skills
Teach people how to get better results from AI.

Examples:
- How to write better prompts
- Prompt structure templates
- Before and after prompt examples
- Common beginner mistakes

### 4. Strengths and Limits
Help users understand both value and risk.

Examples:
- What AI is good at
- Hallucinations and incorrect answers
- Bias and missing context
- When human judgment matters most

### 5. Responsible Use
Teach users to use AI thoughtfully.

Examples:
- Fact checking
- Privacy awareness
- Bias awareness
- Appropriate use in school and work
- Human oversight

### 6. Build With AI
This is for future growth.

Examples:
- Case studies
- Project stories
- Real examples of AI assisted work
- Future integration of projects like Neon Drift

## Content Style Guide

### Voice
Use a tone that is:
- Clear
- Friendly
- Honest
- Practical
- Encouraging
- Professional

### Writing Rules
- Prefer short paragraphs
- Prefer simple headings
- Avoid buzzwords where possible
- Explain terms before building on them
- Use examples often
- Avoid making AI sound human
- Avoid overclaiming what AI can do

### What to Avoid
- Overly technical explanations in the MVP
- Fear based messaging
- Overhyped claims
- Dense wall of text
- Corporate sounding jargon
